Do you have a claim?

You might have money waiting without knowing it

When Mt. Gox collapsed in 2014, the trustee automatically filed claims for many users — even those who never took action. These are labeled Z1- or Z2- followed by a number. If you ever had an account on Mt. Gox, there's a good chance a claim exists in your name.

Bitcoin was worth around $450 when Mt. Gox went down. Today it's worth over $87,000. The trustee held onto the Bitcoin instead of selling, which means even a small balance from 2014 could be worth a significant amount today.

The deadline

October 31, 2026 — then it's over

The Tokyo District Court has extended the repayment deadline three times. The current deadline — October 31, 2026 — is widely expected to be the last. After that date, unclaimed creditors' rights are permanently discharged.

If you haven't completed bank registration, identity verification, and repayment method selection before the deadline, your claim may be forfeited. The process takes weeks, not days — and the trustee's support team is slow. Starting now is not early, it's barely on time.

Common problems

What do you need from me?
Your email and whatever identifying info you have. For the actual recovery: identity documents and authorization forms. We guide you through each step.
What happens if the deadline passes?
Under the rehabilitation plan, unclaimed creditors' rights are permanently discharged after the deadline. The unclaimed funds are redistributed to creditors who completed the process. There have been three extensions — a fourth is not expected.
How much is my claim worth?
It depends on your original balance. The trustee is distributing approximately 21% of approved BTC claims at current market prices. For example, a claim of 10 BTC results in roughly 2.1 BTC — worth over $180,000 at today's prices. We estimate your specific claim value as part of our free assessment.
Can I still file if I never registered?
Many users were automatically registered by the trustee (Z1-/Z2- creditor numbers). Even if you never took action, you may already have an approved claim. We can check this for you.
What if I changed my email or address since 2014?
This is one of the most common issues. The portal requires your current information to match, and updating it involves a specific process through the trustee. We handle these changes regularly.
How long does the process take?
It varies. Simple cases where the claim is approved and just needs bank registration can move in weeks. More complex situations — address changes, identity verification issues, repayment method disputes — can take longer. We give you a realistic timeline after the initial assessment.
Do you handle claims from any country?
Yes. Mt. Gox had users worldwide and the rehabilitation plan covers all approved creditors regardless of location. We've worked with creditors across Europe and are familiar with the cross-border requirements.
